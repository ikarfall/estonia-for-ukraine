Estonia for Ukraine

On this website, you can find information on how to get help if you have been evacuated from or are planning to evacuate to Ukraine. It also provides information on how you could help if you are a resident of Estonia.

The Estonian government is offering assistance to Ukrainians leaving Ukraine

  • Free buses to the Poland-Ukraine border with further transportation to Estonia.
  • Free temporary accommodation in Estonia.
  • Providing essential things.
  • Job search help.
  • Children placement to kindergartens and schools.
  • Taking care of medical issues depending on their occurrence.

I am evacuating and need to be picked up on the border with Ukraine

Step 1

Hotlines to call (Phone, Viber, Telegram):

+372 5350 0595

+372 5350 0208

+372 5350 0609

+372 5420 0809

If you crossed the border in Poland, make a phone call for +48602756657.

Jürgo Loo – the embassy advisor in Poland will help you find transportation.

Other options:

Write your name, phone number, number of people and location at: madle@pagulasabi.ee.

If you have existing relations with Estonia and would like to flee the conflict to Estonia, then please share this information with ukraina@pagulasabi.ee.

You can see the bus times and routes here Estonian Refugee Council and here Ukrainian Diaspora in Estonia.

Step 2

If you have time, please apply for refugee status. You can send an email to asylum@humanrights.ee.

If you want to rent a home by yourself, you may check accommodation availability here www.kv.ee, www.city24.ee.

I am already in Estonia or heading to Estonia by myself – what should I do?

Step 1

Hotlines to call (Phone, Viber, Telegram):

+372 5350 0595

+372 5350 0208

+372 5350 0609

+372 5420 0809

Contact the Embassy of Ukraine in the Republic of Estonia and inform them that you are in Estonia now.

Step 2

If you do not have a temporary place to stay in Estonia, the Department of Social Affairs made a list of companies that are offering discounted hotel services. You can receive information about them by calling 1247 from Estonia or +3726001247 from abroad.

You can also request a temporary asylum here Ukrainian Diaspora in Estonia.

If you want to rent a home by yourself, you may check accommodation availability here www.kv.ee, www.city24.ee.

Step 3

Apply for asylum and international protection by sending an email to asylum@humanrights.ee.

I want to help

Option 1

Buy items from the list below and deliver them to Laboratooriumi, 22, Tallinn (church basement). The collection point starts working on the 28.02.

  • Kevlar helmets;
  • walkie-talkies with trunked radio system (eg Motorola dp4400, Motorola dp4800);
  • bulletproof vests of category 4 and above;
  • karemats, gloves;
  • tactical first aid kits;
  • hemostatic turnstiles;
  • bags for blood;
  • drugs – saline solution, bandages (sterile and non-sterile), syringes 20, 10, 5, 2 ml, tourniquets, hemostatic drugs, analgesics in various forms, drugs for heart disease, stretchers, surgical spirit and disinfectants, gloves and protective masks, antipyretics, sedatives;
  • food of long storage – dry porridges, bars, fast cooked food, canned food;
  • batteries, torches.

If you want to contribute financially, you can send funds to EE847700771005162544, recipient Solomiya Branets. The report will be published on this website.

Option 2

  • Tallinn, Kadaka tee 54 – Punane Ristik store, accepts donations on weekdays from 10:00–18:00 and on Saturdays from 10:00–16:00.
  • to any Selver store throughout Estonia – all stores accept hygiene items, large stores also accept bed linen, blankets, pillows, sleeping bags, towels.
  • Tartu, Ülikooli 18B – every day 10:00–18:00.
